Bandsintown

Bandsintown was created to strengthen the bond between fans and their favorite music artists by simplifying the discovery of upcoming concerts. This valuable relationship between new artist discovery and fan acquisition is created by features that emphasize continuous connection, artist visibility, and direct communication. We decided to evaluate Bandsintown as it’s core mission is very valuable to avid music listeners who frequently miss out on shows they’d otherwise love to see. In the aftermath of COVID-19 people are more excited about getting out to live shows than ever before.

Background

Finding real design experience

Finding information about your favorite and new artists can feel a bit disconnected. You might discover new artists through different social media platforms, buy merch and find tour dates on their personal website, and buy tickets through the venue.

It would be so much easier if all of this functionality was housed in one location. Suppose a potential new fan discovers a new artist on social media, only to become confused about tour dates and where to find current information about the band. These small inconveniences can be the difference between a loyal, lifelong fan and an someone who leaves the website discouraged.

Problem Statement

Music lovers need a place to bring together social media and music discovery through following artists they are passionate about and having new ones suggested, allowing them to follow current content, tour dates, and ticket prices.

Intended Users

The Busy New Grad: Jess

Jess is a 23 year old Mechanical Engineer from Chicago, Illinios. She loves live music, but she feels like by the time she hears that her favorite artist is coming to Chicago, the tickets have already been scalped and they now cost 3x the original price.

Jess wants a more efficient way to keep up with live music opportunities, as it is hard to remember to check the instagram of every artist she is interested in.

The Die Hard Fan: Ryan

Ryan is a 20 year old student who is a server at a restaurant, living in Los Angeles, California. He moved to Los Angeles to be closer to a large music scene, typically attending multiple shows a month.

Ryan wants to find a way to show the smaller bands that he loves that he is interested in having them come to his city. He also wants a way to discover new smaller artists to see live that he has not yet heard of.

User Testing

Scenario 1

Try to find a new artist and add them to your following list.

Scenario 2

Browse through the artists that you follow and search for any upcoming shows that they are playing.

Scenario 1

Where Our Users Experienced Problems

Through our user testing, we found that users were confused as to how to use the "Browse by Artist" page to add new artists. They thought that to search for a new artist, they would go to this page, but there is no search bar. You can only be given recommended artists, and there is no way to filter your results. You are just given a random assortment of faceless artist names. They were both unable to search for new artists and unable to tell what kinds of random artists they were being recommended.

Our Redesign's Solution

For our redesign, we chose to create an explore section that is more intuitive for the users. The original version had users guessing where to go to search for new artists. The ambiguously named section called "Browse by Artist" no longer draws them in and there is a clear search bar labeled "Explore" that has an obvoius search filter icon next to it, giving users a clear path to find new artists in a geanre that they enjoy.

Scenario 2

Where Our Users Experienced Problems

We found that users were having a very difficult time locating the artists that they were currently following. The "Browse by Artist" section seemed to them like a place where you would go to find new artists, but that is where most of our users ended up eventually finding the artists that they follow. The default view of the page has the slider at the top set to recommended, so they are being given a selection of random artists along with the ones that they follow. They were confused how to set it to just show the artists that they follow because the bar at the top appears to be a continuous slider bar, but it operates as a switch.

Our Redesign's Solution

The majority of our users said that they would be using this app primarily to follow artists they like to be able to easily tell when they are playing shows in their area. This is why it is so important that we make it clear where you can go to see your artists. The redesign that we implemented makes it clear that there is a section for "Your Artists" instead of one section called "Browse by Artist" that our users found to be very ambiguous. We also made sure that the artists being shown had visual elements to them instead of the original version that only showed your followed artists as faceless names.

Reflections

My Biggest Takeaways

This prototype allowed for much more ease of access to different sections of the page due to the addition of horizontal and vertical scrolling. The added negative space made it much more approachable to new users as well. You also have the ability to browse by genres and set your location, but you can also browse by your favorite artists or emerging artists. This gives you quick access to your favorite musicians and makes it very easy to explore new ones. However, it lacks the customizability to create a custom feed where you can have a blend of updates from artists, new recommendations, and shows in your area, allowing you to truly explore. Additionally, it lacks the ability to select the specific date you want to find shows for.
