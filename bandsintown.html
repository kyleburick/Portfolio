Bandsintown

Bandsintown was created to strengthen the bond between fans and their favorite music artists by simplifying the discovery of upcoming concerts. This valuable relationship between new artist discovery and fan acquisition is created by features that emphasize continuous connection, artist visibility, and direct communication. We decided to evaluate Bandsintown, looking at its success in achieving its core mission of serving as a valuable tool to help avid music listeners who frequently miss out on shows they’d otherwise love to see.

Background

Finding real design experience

Finding information about your favorite artists and discovering new artists can feel a bit disconnected. You might discover new artists through different social media platforms, buy merch and find tour dates on their personal website, and buy tickets through the concert venue.

It would be so much easier if all of this functionality was housed in one location. Suppose a potential new fan discovers a new artist on social media, only to become confused about tour dates and where to find current information about the band. These small inconveniences can be the difference between a loyal, lifelong fan, and an someone who leaves the website discouraged.

Problem Statement

Music lovers need a place to bring together social media and music discovery through following artists they are passionate about and having new ones suggested, allowing them to follow current content, tour dates, and ticket prices.

Intended Users

The Busy New Grad: Jess

Jess is a 23 year old Mechanical Engineer from Chicago, Illinios. She loves live music, but she feels like by the time she hears that her favorite artist is coming to Chicago, the tickets have already been scalped and they now cost 3x the original price.

Jess wants a more efficient way to keep up with live music opportunities, as it is hard to remember to check the instagram of every artist she is interested in.

The Die Hard Fan: Ryan

Ryan is a 20 year old student who is a server at a restaurant, living in Los Angeles, California. He moved to Los Angeles to be closer to a large music scene, typically attending multiple shows a month.

Ryan wants to find a way to show the smaller bands that he loves that he is interested in having them come to his city. He also wants a way to discover new smaller artists to see live that he has not yet heard of.

User Testing

Scenario 1

Try to find a new artist and add them to your following list.

Scenario 2

Browse through the artists that you follow and search for any upcoming shows that they are playing.

Scenario 1

Where Our Users Experienced Problems

Through our user testing, we found that users were confused about how to use the "Browse by Artist" page to add new artists. They thought that to search for a new artist, they would go to this page, but this page had no search bar. Instead, this page only gave them recommended artists, without a way to filter results. The user is just given a random assortment of faceless artist names. They were both unable to search for new artists and unable to determine what kinds of random artists the app was recommending.

Our Redesign's Solution

For our redesign, we chose to eliminate the confusing “Browse by Artist” section in favor of a new “Explore” section that is more intuitive for the users. The original version had users guessing where to go to search for new artists. The ambiguously named section, called "Browse by Artist," no longer draws them in and there is a clear search bar labeled "Explore" that has an obvoius search filter icon next to it, giving users a clear path to find new artists in a genre that they enjoy.

Scenario 2

Where Our Users Experienced Problems

We found that users were having a very difficult time locating the artists that they were currently following. The "Browse by Artist" section seemed to them like the place to go to find new artists and shows. The default view of the “Browse by Artist” page has the slider at the top, set to recommended, so the user is being given a selection of random artists along with the ones that the user follows. The users were confused how to set the app to just show the artists that they follow because the bar at the top appears to be a continuous slider bar, but it operates as a switch.

Our Redesign's Solution

The user research data showed that the majority of the users said that they would be using this app primarily to follow artists they liked in order to be notified of local shows for these artists in their area. This is why it is so important that we make it clear where the user can go to see favorite artists. The redesign that we implemented adds a section for "Your Artists" instead of the ambiguous "Browse by Artist" section. We also made sure that each displayed artist had identifying visual elements instead of the faceless names for followed artists in the original version.

Reflections

My Biggest Takeaways

The redesigned prototype allowed for greater ease of access to different sections of the page with the addition of horizontal and vertical scrolling. The added negative space made the app much more approachable to new users as well. A users now also has the ability to browse by genres and set a location, and the user can also browse by favorite artists or emerging artists. This gives the user quick access to favorite musicians and makes it very easy to explore new ones. However, it needs additional work as it still lacks the customizability to create a custom feed where the user can have a blend of information, including updates from artists, new recommendations, and shows in the user’s area, allowing a user to truly explore. Additionally, it still lacks the ability to search for shows by a specific date.
